Executive Summary

Horizon Robotics is a leading Chinese provider of computing solutions for advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) and autonomous driving. Founded in 2015 by Dr. Kai Yu, former head of Baidu's Institute of Deep Learning, the company designs AI processors (Journey series) and provides full-stack software solutions for intelligent vehicles. Listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange in October 2024, Horizon Robotics has become one of China's most prominent domestic alternatives to foreign autonomous driving chip suppliers.

Investment Thesis

Horizon Robotics occupies a strategic position in China's autonomous driving ecosystem, benefiting from: (1) government push for semiconductor self-sufficiency amid US-China tech decoupling, (2) rapid adoption of ADAS in Chinese NEVs, (3) deep partnerships with major OEMs including Li Auto, BYD, and Volkswagen, and (4) competitive technology with the Journey 5/6 series chips. However, the company remains loss-making with significant R&D burn, faces intense competition from Nvidia and domestic players, and operates in a capital-intensive industry with long product development cycles.

Round 1: Market Position and Growth Trajectory

BULL

Horizon Robotics is positioned at the epicenter of three powerful secular trends: EV adoption in China (35%+ NEV penetration), ADAS democratization (55% penetration today heading to 85% by 2030), and semiconductor localization amid US-China tech decoupling. Revenue growth has been exceptional - 92% in 2022, 71% in 2023. Their customer list reads like a who's who of Chinese automotive. With 7+ million cumulative chip shipments, this is no longer speculative - it's a proven growth company in a $100B market by 2030.

BEAR

The growth narrative conveniently ignores the elephant in the room: Horizon Robotics is burning cash at an alarming rate with no clear path to profitability. They lost RMB 6.7 billion in 2023 and are projected to lose another RMB 5.5 billion in 2024. R&D expenses alone were 155% of revenue. Even with IPO proceeds, they're looking at 3+ years of cash burn before reaching breakeven in 2027 - and that's the optimistic scenario. Meanwhile, Nvidia still commands 55% market share despite export restrictions.

SYNTHESIZER

Round 1 reveals a fundamental tension between Horizon's strong market positioning and its challenging financial reality. The Bull correctly identifies real tailwinds. The Bear rightly highlights that growth without profitability is ultimately unsustainable. The current evidence suggests a middle path - L2+ adoption is robust and provides a solid base business, while L3/L4 represents optionality rather than a base case assumption.

Round 2: Competitive Dynamics and Technology Moat

BULL

Unlike Nvidia which sells chips, Horizon provides 'chip + algorithm + software' - a complete solution. The proprietary BPU architecture delivers industry-leading TOPS/Watt efficiency. Once an OEM integrates Horizon's stack, switching costs are extremely high - these are multi-year design cycles. The Volkswagen CARIAD investment of over $1 billion is validation from a global OEM that Horizon's technology is world-class.

BEAR

Horizon faces pressure from above (Nvidia) and below (domestic players). Black Sesame's A2000 chip targets the same segment. Huawei's MDC platform is backed by one of China's most powerful tech companies. Even more concerning, major OEMs like BYD are developing in-house chip capabilities. The 70% gross margin is impressive but unsustainable in a commoditizing market.

SYNTHESIZER

Horizon occupies a defensible middle ground: more integrated than Nvidia, more established than domestic alternatives, and more neutral than Huawei. The most likely scenario is market segmentation: Nvidia retains premium tier, Horizon dominates mainstream Chinese OEM tier, while Black Sesame and others compete on cost. This positioning supports 15-20% market share but caps upside.

Round 4: Geopolitical Factors and China Tech Sentiment

BULL

Geopolitical risk is precisely why Horizon Robotics is positioned to win. Every new US chip restriction accelerates Chinese OEM adoption of domestic alternatives. Nvidia's China automotive revenue is now structurally constrained. The VW CARIAD investment demonstrates that even Western OEMs recognize the strategic imperative of having a China-based supplier. The 'China tech discount' creates entry opportunities for patient investors.

BEAR

The geopolitical 'tailwind' is a double-edged sword. Horizon designs chips on advanced process nodes (7nm) that currently only TSMC can manufacture at scale. If the US extends export controls to cover Chinese autonomous driving chip companies - as they did with AI chips - Horizon could find itself cut off from TSMC entirely. Huawei's HiSilicon was the world's leading mobile chip designer before US sanctions effectively killed the business. Horizon is one executive order away from the same fate.

SYNTHESIZER

Horizon is a play on 'China tech sovereignty' - benefiting from localization trends while facing concentration risk. Key nuance: autonomous driving chips face lower sanction risk than datacenter AI chips. Most likely scenario is continued tension without complete decoupling - a 'cold tech war' where Horizon thrives domestically but cannot expand internationally. TSMC risk is real but manageable.
