    1. BUSINESS MODEL

    Revenue Breakdown by Segment/Product: Legend Biotech primarily generates revenue through its flagship product, Carvykti (ciltacabtagene autoleucel), a CAR-T cell therapy for the treatment of multiple myeloma. As of the latest available data, Carvykti is the primary revenue driver post its commercial launch.

    Revenue Breakdown by Geography: The revenue is largely concentrated in North America due to its partnership with Janssen Pharmaceuticals, which co-markets Carvykti in the U.S. and other major markets. Expansion into European and Asian markets is anticipated following regulatory approvals.

    Recurring vs One-Time Revenue: The revenue model for Carvykti incorporates both one-time sales from the initial therapy and potential recurring revenues from follow-up treatments or maintenance therapies. However, the primary revenue from CAR-T therapies is typically one-time upon initial administration.

    Unit Economics: Unit economics for CAR-T therapies are influenced by high production costs, given the personalized nature of the treatment, which requires harvesting, modifying, and re-infusing a patient's own cells. The gross margin is expected to improve as production scales and processes become more efficient.

    Customer Concentration: Legend Biotech's customer base is heavily concentrated within major healthcare providers and hospitals that administer CAR-T therapies. The partnership with Janssen also implies a significant reliance on their distribution and sales networks.

    Pricing Power: Carvykti's pricing power is robust due to its innovative treatment capabilities and efficacy in addressing a critical unmet need in multiple myeloma treatment. However, pricing is also subject to negotiations with healthcare payers and insurers.

    2. COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGES (MOAT)

    Brand Value: Carvykti has established a significant brand presence in the CAR-T therapy space, supported by clinical efficacy and strategic partnerships.

    Network Effects: While network effects are limited due to the nature of the industry, partnerships with established players like Janssen enhance market reach and credibility.

    Switching Costs: High switching costs exist for patients once treatment with Carvykti has commenced, as alternative therapies would require restarting the treatment process.

    Cost Advantages: Currently, cost advantages are limited due to high production costs. However, potential improvements in manufacturing efficiencies could enhance cost competitiveness.

    Intangible Assets: Legend Biotech holds several patents related to its CAR-T technologies, providing a significant competitive edge in protecting its innovations.

    Efficient Scale: The market for multiple myeloma therapies is large but highly specialized, allowing Legend Biotech to benefit from economies of scale as adoption of Carvykti increases.
